# Life OS System Constitution

**Document ID:** LOS-CON-001

**Status:** Draft

**Owner:** Architecture Team

---

# Purpose

This document defines the identity, philosophy, scope, and immutable governing rules of the Life OS project.

It is the highest authority within the repository.

Every document, architectural decision, specification, implementation, and test must comply with this constitution.

---

# Identity

Life OS is a Personal Operating System Platform.

It is designed to organize, operate, monitor, automate, and continuously improve every manageable aspect of an individual's life through a specification-driven, layered, and modular architecture.

---

# Vision

Build a long-term personal operating platform capable of managing all life domains through structured information, automation, and continuous improvement.

---

# Mission

Transform daily life into an organized, measurable, and continuously improving system.

---

# Scope

Life OS manages personal information, activities, goals, habits, knowledge, work, learning, health, finance, relationships, and any future life domain through independent modules operating under one unified architecture.

---

# Non-Scope

Life OS does not replace every external application.

External systems may be integrated whenever they provide better capabilities than rebuilding them.

---

# Core Objectives

- Organize life.
- Reduce cognitive load.
- Preserve knowledge.
- Measure progress.
- Automate repetitive work.
- Generate meaningful insights.
- Support continuous improvement.
- Remain modular and extensible.

---

# Constitutional Rules

- Documentation before implementation.
- Architecture before code.
- Specification before implementation.
- Layered architecture.
- Modular design.
- Explicit contracts.
- Single source of truth.
- Test before completion.
- Long-term maintainability.
- Replaceability of components.

---

# Amendment

This constitution may only change when the fundamental identity or philosophy of Life OS changes.
